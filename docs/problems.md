MCP23017 has no ESP-IDF native library. The common Arduino library (Adafruit_MCP23017) won't work under ESP-IDF. You'll either write a small custom I2C driver yourself (it's just register reads/writes, maybe 50 lines) or find a community ESP-IDF component. Same situation as HX711 — flagging it again here because it's a second instance of the same gap, not a one-off.
ESP-IDF has no servo.write(angle) equivalent. You'll calculate LEDC PWM duty cycle values manually to get the standard 50Hz/1-2ms pulse servos expect. Solvable in a few lines, just not as friendly as Arduino — write yourself a small servo_set_angle() wrapper once and reuse it for all 4 hopper dosers.
L298N has a real voltage drop (~2V). It's an older bipolar-transistor driver, not MOSFET-based. Feed it 12V and your actuators effectively see closer to 9-10V — they'll run slightly under their rated speed/force. Not fatal, just don't be confused later when they seem a bit underpowered; that's the driver, not a wiring fault.
Don't connect the L298N's onboard 5V regulator output to anything. Most L298N modules include a small 5V regulator that can generate logic-level power from the 12V input — if you accidentally wire that output into your separate breadboard 5V rail, you're bridging two power domains you specifically want kept apart. Only use the 12V input terminal, the IN1-IN4 logic pins, and ground.
Check your 5V supply's current rating before running all 4 hopper servos together. SG90s can spike to several hundred mA each under load; four at once briefly could approach 1-2A. A basic USB-powered breadboard supply may not have that headroom — worth staggering servo fires slightly rather than commanding all four simultaneously, at least until you've confirmed your supply handles it.
